Fathom - A New Way To Get Organized

I aim to help people organize their thoughts, plans, and projects by providing them with a unique diagramming tool built with a focus on minimalism and integrated scripting. After all the projects I've worked on, whether it be for a hobby or for work, I have naturally gravitated towards diagrams because of the way they let me organize ideas and plans in a highly visual way. Unfortunately, I've been disappointed by every diagramming tool I've ever used. Current diagramming tools try to be the end-all-be-all solution by providing every possible feature.

The tool I used to use had enough features to essentially draw any diagram you could think of, but it came with a price: the interface was ugly, cluttered, and confusing. And it didn't provide a way to create any clean and organized diagrams right off the bat. For someone who's trying to make diagrams for other people to look at, that amount of control might actually be useful.

But for me, an individual making simple diagrams for my own reference, I needed a tool that wasn't weighed down by a zoo of features. I needed a tool that was carefully tuned to provide the most stream-lined interface for getting my thoughts out of my head and into a diagram.

That was when I invented Fathom. This diagramming tool is designed to be fast and efficient. One of my primary goals is for Fathom to be able to open a diagram file without any noticable delay. Another goal is for the interface to put all the attention on the diagram without filling valuable screen space with an endless variety of menus. The clean and intuitive interface also avoids the finicky frustration other diagram tools cause by their excess of buttons and pop-ups. To top it all off, Fathom allows users to write small scripts directly in their diagrams that can automate the sorts of calculations that would normally be done in a spreadsheet, which makes Fathom the best choice for managing information and data at a personal level. Overall, Fathom is the best tool for recording, organizing, and structuring ideas and information in an intuitive way.
